Why it matters

Since the 1900's, the the temperature of each country has experienced around 1°C of warming. This may not appear to be a big deal since it's just 1°C, however this causes deadly problems. Hot countries will face longer and more frequent heat waves causing droughts. The increase in temperature will also kill off crops, plants and animal species, and the coral reefs. The melting of the polar ice caps will cause a sea level rise which affects small islands the most. The coral reefs won't be able to protect the marine animals and humans that rely on the reefs for protection.

At 1.5°C, coral reefs have a chance at adapting and recovering, however at 2°C, the chance of recovering disappears. The Mediterranean is forecasted to have 9% less water at 1.5°C and 18% less water at 2°C. Sea levels will rise due to ice sheets and glaciers being heated from above and under from the increase in ocean temperatures.
