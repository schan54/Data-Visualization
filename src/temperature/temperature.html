The Effect of a Warming Planet

Since the 1900's, the the temperature of each country has experienced around 1°C of warming. This may not appear to be a big deal since it's just 1°C, however this causes deadly problems. Hot countries will face longer and more frequent heat waves causing droughts.


The increase in temperature will also kill off crops, plants and animal species, and the coral reefs. The melting of the polar ice caps will cause a sea level rise which affects small islands the most. The coral reefs won't be able to protect the marine animals and humans that rely on the reefs for protection.

Vizualizing Anomalies

For example, In Russia you can see a dramatic spike in temperature from the years 1994 to 1995, however the following years from 1995 to 1996 you see a dramatic decrease in temperature. And right after this decrease, the temperature continues to rise.

The Impact On Our Oceans

At 1.5°C, coral reefs have a chance at adapting and recovering, however at 2°C, the chance of recovering disappears. The Mediterranean is forecasted to have 9% less water at 1.5°C and 18% less water at 2°C. Since the ocean holds so much heat, sea levels will rise due to ice sheets and glaciers being heated from above and under from the increase in ocean temperatures.

What Does This Mean For Us?

At 1.5°C, the production of wheat goes down 9% and corn production goes down 3%. At 2°C, the production of wheat goes down 16% and corn production goes down 6%. Although the production of soy and rice goes up 6% at 1.5°C and 2°C, if you get high temperatures or very dry conditions during the development of crops, it produces essentially no grain. At certain temperature thresholds, corn doesn't die but it also doesn't grow any seeds which defeats the purpose of growing corn. The benefits of fertilization with more carbon dioxide is offsetted by the fact that plant will still die from the temperature and dry conditions. A plant growing really fast when it died is still a dead plant. There is no benefits for crops with an increase in temperature.


Faced with this reality, we must understand where to go from this. We should try our best to limit this half degree growth, however we must be very careful since we have little experience with limiting glboal warming with precision. If we try to limit growth to 1.5°C, it may end up being 2°C or 2°C may end up being 3°C. If we were to exceed this 1.5°C limit and then brought the temperature back down, there is no guarantee that the consequences of crossing this limit would be reversed.

Is It Too Late?

If you compare the temperature of 1901 France with 2014 France, you will notice how far we have pushed our limits. You can see that there has been an increase of 1.36°C over the past century. Although the warmer months haven't seen much increase, the cooler months have seen a signifcant rise in temperature.
