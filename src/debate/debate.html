The Skepticism

Out of 29,000 scientists, 97.1% of scientists agreed that climate change is caused by human activity. 2.2% of the remaining scientists were unclear while 0.7% of the scientists disputed that climate change is caused by human activity. Compared to the 97% agreement by scientists, public opinions are not nearly as close to an agreement.

In 2014, the Public Religion Research Institute conducted a survey of 3,022 random adults for their thoughts on climate change. Of the 3,022 adults, 23% were skeptical of climate change. More than half of the skeptics believed they don't notice any changes in weather and if they did, it's due to a natural cycle where temperature goes up or down every couple of hundred years. 12% of skeptics don't believe due to insufficient evidence.

With these reasonings in mind, we hope to change their minds.

Our Response

Our visuals show that these skeptics are incorrect about their belief that climate change isn't related to human activity. Our temperature visual and choropleth shows that the world is getting warmer. Not all countries experience the same effects of rise in temperature. Certain countries experience this rise more than others.

Our energy and carbon dioxide visuals show that the temperature isn't varying naturally. Humans are using more energy and producing more carbon dioxide causing the rise in temperature.

Our datasets are sourced from credible sources with experience in gathering data from their fields. For example, our energy source, Enerdata, is experienced in energy markets and international energy coverages.
