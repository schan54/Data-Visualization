Rising Seas

As temperature increases and glaciers melt, sea levels also increase. Since the 1880's, sea levels have risen 16-21 cm, with 7.5 cm being within the last 25 years. These higher sea levels have been causing increasingly dangerous hurricanes and typhoons, flooding, soil erosion, and damage to city infrastructure.

How is sea level rise affecting the infrastructure of cities?

About 40% of the United States' population, and 10% of the world's population, lives in coastal cities. As sea levels continue to rise, coastal urban life has been affected. Approximately 30% of the city of Manhattan, New York is expected to be fully submerged in water by 2050. Currently, the city of Venice, Italy is experiencing some of its worst flooding. In 2018, approximately 75% of the city was flooded. With the current rates of sea level rise, it is predicted that the Maldives would be inhabitable by 2100 due to flooding caused by rising sea levels.

What causes sea levels to rise?

Temperature

Rising temperatures are warming ocean waters, which expand as the temperature increases. As the ocean waters expand, they take up more space, causing the sea levels to rise.


Glacial Melting

The melting of land glaciers is one of the two main causes of sea level rise. As the glaciers melt, their water drips off into the ocean, adding more volume. Areas with the biggest concern of glacial melting include Antarctica and Greenland, as they host many of the world's land glaciers.
